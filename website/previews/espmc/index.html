The Physician

A physician who has practiced from the same address for over two decades does not need volume to survive.

Dr. Daniel P. Klein, MD, FACP is a Fellow of the American College of Physicians — a distinction held by fewer than 30% of practicing internists in the United States. He has been at 79th Street since 2001.

His practice is not managed by a hospital network, a private equity group, or an insurance panel. It is his practice. That distinction matters in medicine more than anywhere else.

The Concierge Choice Program offers his members a different experience entirely — limited appointment volume, direct physician access, and continuity of care that the standard system is structurally incapable of providing.

The Concierge Programme

What private medicine
actually means.

01

Two Patients Per Hour

Not eight. Not twelve. Two. Every appointment is long enough for medicine to happen properly — examination, history, decision-making, unhurried.

02

Same-Day Access

Same-day or next-day appointments, always. A private direct line to a Concierge Coordinator — not a call centre, not a portal, not a three-week wait.

03

After-Hours Coverage

Evening and weekend physician availability. When something happens outside of a Tuesday at 11am, you still have access to your doctor.

04

Annual Comprehensive Exam

A full screening examination that goes far beyond the standard annual physical — the baseline for everything else in your care plan.

05

National Travel Coverage

A network of concierge physicians covering you when you are not in New York. Your standard of care does not change because your zip code does.

06

Membership, Not Transactions

Concierge medicine is a relationship. Dr. Klein knows your history, your family, your risk profile. That knowledge does not reset between visits.
